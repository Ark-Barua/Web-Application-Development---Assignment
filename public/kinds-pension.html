Kinds of Pension

Overview

The government provides various types of pension benefits to its employees based on different circumstances and service conditions. Understanding these different kinds of pension is essential for proper planning and application.

Types of Pension

Superannuation Pension

Pension granted to employees who retire on attaining the age of superannuation (60 years) or after completing the required years of service.

Eligibility:
  • Minimum 10 years of qualifying service
  • Attainment of superannuation age
  • Valid service record

Family Pension

Pension granted to the family members of a deceased government employee who dies while in service or after retirement.

Eligibility:
  • Spouse and dependent children
  • Parents (in specific cases)
  • Valid nomination and documents

Invalid Pension

Pension granted to employees who are permanently incapacitated for further service due to bodily or mental infirmity.

Eligibility:
  • Medical board certification
  • Minimum 10 years of service
  • Permanent disability assessment

Voluntary Retirement Pension

Pension granted to employees who opt for voluntary retirement after completing the minimum required years of service.

Eligibility:
  • Minimum 20 years of service
  • Age 50 years or above
  • Departmental approval

Compensation Pension

Pension granted to employees who are discharged from service due to abolition of post or reduction in establishment.

Eligibility:
  • Minimum 5 years of service
  • No alternative employment offered
  • Administrative decision

Retiring Pension

Pension granted to employees who retire after completing the required years of service but before attaining superannuation age.

Eligibility:
  • Minimum 20 years of service
  • Age 50 years or above
  • Departmental permission

Special Categories

War Injury Pension

Special pension for employees who suffer injuries during war or war-like operations.

Disability Pension

Pension for employees who become disabled due to service-related injuries or accidents.

Extraordinary Pension

Pension granted in special circumstances as per government orders and regulations.

Pension Calculation Methods

Defined Benefit Method

Traditional method where pension is calculated as a percentage of the last pay drawn.

New Pension Scheme (NPS)

Contributory pension scheme where both employee and government contribute to the pension fund.

Hybrid Method

Combination of defined benefit and defined contribution methods for specific categories.

Important Considerations

  • Service verification is mandatory for all pension types
  • Medical examination required for disability pensions
  • Family pension is payable to only one person at a time
  • Pension amount is subject to periodic revisions
  • Tax implications vary based on pension type
  • Nomination is crucial for family pension

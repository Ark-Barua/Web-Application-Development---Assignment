General Provident Fund (GPF)

What is GPF?

The General Provident Fund (GPF) is a compulsory savings scheme for government employees in India. It is a long-term investment option that helps employees build a corpus for their retirement.

Key Features

Interest Rate

Currently 7.1% per annum (compounded quarterly)

Contribution

6% of basic pay + DA (compulsory contribution)

Withdrawal

Available for specific purposes with conditions

Security

Government guaranteed, risk-free investment

GPF Services

Account Management

  • Opening new GPF accounts
  • Maintenance of GPF accounts
  • Annual statements
  • Account balance verification

Advances & Withdrawals

  • GPF advances for specific purposes
  • Final withdrawal on retirement
  • Premature withdrawal (with conditions)
  • Nomination services

Online Services

  • GPF account balance check
  • Download annual statements
  • Track contribution history
  • Apply for advances online

Eligibility

The following categories of employees are eligible for GPF:

  • Permanent government employees
  • Employees on probation (after completion of probation)
  • Re-employed pensioners (subject to conditions)
  • Contract employees (as per specific rules)

Important Guidelines

Contribution Rules

  • Minimum contribution: 6% of basic pay + DA
  • Maximum contribution: 100% of basic pay + DA
  • Contribution is deducted monthly from salary
  • Interest is credited quarterly

Withdrawal Rules

  • Available for specific purposes only
  • Maximum 12 months' pay or 3/4th of balance
  • Repayment within 24 months
  • Final withdrawal on retirement/death
